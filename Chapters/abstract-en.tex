This thesis aims to develop a dynamic, high-performance communication network capable of countering state-of-the-art correlation attacks to ensure user anonymity and privacy, all while maintaining practical levels of throughput and latency. This project uses an existing platform leveraging K-anonymization and WebRTC-based TLS tunnels for covert communication, with advanced multipath routing and differential privacy (DP) circuits. By integrating differential privacy principles, this work aims to inject calibrated noise into traffic flows and statistical communication patterns, mitigating the risk of privacy leakage even under advanced traffic analysis and censorship attacks.

Unlike traditional anonymity networks like Tor, which often face a trade-off between performance and formal privacy guarantees, our approach will use DP’s mathematically rigorous framework to provide strong privacy assurances without severely compromising network performance. Tor and similar networks prioritize either formal security with slow performance or faster operation with weaker security guarantees, leaving gaps for potential deanonymization under sophisticated attack techniques. This thesis will explore and evaluate how DP circuits can enhance the anonymity networks, creating an adaptable, privacy-preserving communication environment that offers a viable alternative for real-world applications requiring both robust anonymity and communication.
