We connect to the internet daily, gaining access to an overwhelming flow of information about the world. 
Freedom of speech is an essential right, assumed as an unquestionable part of our daily lives. 
However, this right is denied by authoritarian regimes and entities that control the flow of information, in order to maintain power and control over their citizens. 
These forces have continuously advanced their methods and resources to enforce such oppressive policies more effectively.
Therefore, it is crucial to ensure that those living under such oppression gain access to the tools and content denied to them and have the means to communicate freely, securely, and without fear.

To tackle this challenge, we aim to develop a communication network, on top of Tor network, capable of countering state-of-the-art correlation attacks to ensure user anonymity and privacy.
By incorporating Differential Privacy principles, the solution introduces carefully calibrated noise into traffic flow and ensures robust and formally proven privacy guarantees, distinguishing itself from solutions that rely on practical testing. Furthermore, Differential Privacy also provides customizable and adjustable privacy controls, empowering users to balance the trade-off between privacy and utility to their needs.
